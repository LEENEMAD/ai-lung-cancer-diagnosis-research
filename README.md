#  AI & Big Data for Early Lung Cancer Diagnosis

##  Overview
This project explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Big Data to improve early lung cancer diagnosis, with a focus on the Jordanian healthcare system.

It combines **academic research**, **real-world insights**, and **machine learning implementation** to analyze challenges, ethical considerations, and the effectiveness of predictive models.

---

##  Objectives
- Investigate how AI can improve early lung cancer detection  
- Identify challenges in integrating AI into healthcare systems  
- Address ethical and legal concerns in Jordan  
- Evaluate machine learning models for prediction  

---

##  Project Components

###  1. Research Study
The research focuses on:
- Ethical concerns (patient consent, data privacy)  
- Legal regulations in Jordan  
- Integration challenges in hospitals  
- Impact of AI on healthcare decision-making  

Primary data was collected through surveys with healthcare professionals and data specialists.

---

###  2. Machine Learning Implementation
A practical ML system was developed to predict lung cancer using patient data.

#### Models Used:
- K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN)  
- Decision Tree  
- Random Forest  

#### Evaluation Metrics:
- Accuracy  
- Precision  
- Recall  
- F1-score  

---

##  Results
- Random Forest achieved the best overall performance  
- AI models showed strong potential for early diagnosis  
- Highlighted the importance of high accuracy in medical applications  

---

##  Key Insights
- Patient data privacy and consent are major concerns  
- Integration into existing healthcare systems is challenging  
- AI should support doctors, not replace them  
- Trust and transparency are critical for adoption
